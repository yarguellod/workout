Pike Pulses Seated ‚ñº

üßò‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è PIKE POSITION

How to: Sit with legs straight in front of you. Place hands on floor beside or in front of you. Lean forward 1-2 inches and pulse back and forth. Keep movements small and controlled - builds muscle endurance in pike position.

Reps: 2 sets x 20 pulses

Seated Pike Compressions ‚ñº

üí™ COMPRESSION

How to: Sit in pike position (legs straight). Hands beside hips on floor. Lean forward and actively try to lift heels 1 inch off ground. Hold 5 seconds with MAXIMUM engagement. This builds active flexibility!

Reps: 5 x 5 second holds

Standing Pike (Bent Knees) ‚ñº

ü¶µ FORWARD FOLD

How to: Stand and forward fold. Bend knees generously so hands touch floor. Now slowly try to straighten legs while keeping hands on floor. Bend knees again. Repeat slowly 10 times. Builds hamstring strength in stretched position.

Reps: 10 slow reps

Elevated L-sit (Knees Bent) ‚ñº

üî∫ L-POSITION

How to: Use parallettes, yoga blocks, or push-up bars. Sit between them with hands on equipment. Press down hard through hands and lift your butt off the ground. Keep knees bent. Hold 10 seconds. Builds shoulder and core strength!

Reps: 5 x 10 second holds

Push-up to Down Dog ‚ñº

‚¨ÜÔ∏è DOWN DOG

How to: Start in plank position (push-up position). Slowly push your hips up and back toward down dog position - take 5 full seconds. Focus on actively pulling your belly toward your thighs (active compression!). Return to plank. Repeat 10 times.

Reps: 10 slow transitions

Hollow Body Hold ‚ñº

üî• CORE ENGAGED

How to: Lie on back. Press lower back into floor (NO ARCH!). Lift shoulders and bent knees off ground. Arms can be overhead or by sides. Keep core SUPER tight. This is essential for toes to bar!

Reps: 3 x 20 seconds

Wall Handstand Hold ‚ñº

üôÉ INVERTED

How to: Face the wall. Place hands on floor about 6 inches from wall. Kick up so feet rest on wall. Push ACTIVELY through shoulders (don't just hang there!). Keep body tight and straight. Builds handstand endurance and shoulder strength.

Duration: Varies by day (20-30 seconds per set)

Pike Push-ups ‚ñº

üìê PIKE POSITION

How to: Start in pike position (hips high, forming upside-down V). Place hands on floor or on yoga blocks for more range. Lower your head toward floor between your hands. Push back up. Keep hips HIGH throughout! Builds pressing strength for handstand.

Reps: Varies by day (4-7 reps per set)

Plank to Pike ‚ñº

‚ö° DYNAMIC

How to: Start in plank position. Push hips up high into pike position (like upside-down V). Return to plank. Keep legs as straight as possible. Builds compression strength and shoulder mobility. Focus on controlled movement.

Reps: 4 x 8-10 reps

Hanging Knee Raises ‚ñº

üéØ HANGING

How to: Hang from pull-up bar with straight arms. Engage your core and lift knees toward chest. HOLD at the top for 3 seconds. Lower with control. NO SWINGING! This builds the foundation for toes to bar.

Reps: 4 x 6-8 reps with 3 sec hold

Lying Leg Raises ‚ñº

‚¨ÜÔ∏è LEGS UP

How to: Lie on back with legs straight. Press lower back into floor (no arch!). Slowly raise legs to 90 degrees. Lower with 5-second control. If back arches, stop before legs reach floor. Builds core strength for toes to bar.

Reps: 4 x 8-10 reps

Dead Hang ‚ñº

üí™ GRIP STRENGTH

How to: Simply hang from bar with straight arms. Engage shoulders by pulling shoulder blades down slightly (active hang). Builds grip strength and shoulder stability. Essential foundation! Work up to 60+ seconds.

Duration: 3 x 30-40 seconds

Toes to Bar (GOAL) ‚ñº

üéØ GOAL MOVEMENT

How to: Hang from bar. Using pure core strength (NO swinging or kipping), bring your toes up to touch the bar between your hands. Lower with control. THIS IS YOUR GOAL! Start by just getting toes as high as you can and progress over time.

Goal: 20+ strict reps by end of 6 months

Box Pistol Squats ‚ñº

ü™ë ASSISTED

How to: Stand on one leg in front of a box or chair. Other leg extends straight in front. Lower SLOWLY (5 seconds) to sit on box with control. Stand back up on same leg. Start with chair height, progress to lower boxes over time.

Reps: 4 x 6-8 per leg

Assisted Pistol Squats ‚ñº

ü§ù SUPPORT

How to: Hold TRX straps, rings, or doorframe for balance. Perform pistol squat while holding support lightly. Use support only for balance - try to do most of the work with your legs. Reduce support over time as you get stronger.

Reps: 4 x 6-8 per leg

Full ROM Pistol Squat (GOAL) ‚ñº

üéØ GOAL MOVEMENT

How to: Stand on one leg. Other leg extends straight in front. Lower ALL the way down until thigh touches calf. Stand back up on same leg. Other leg stays straight throughout. THIS IS YOUR GOAL! Takes months to build the strength and mobility for this.

Goal: 4 x 10 perfect pistols by end of 6 months

Single-leg Romanian Deadlifts ‚ñº

ü¶µ HAMSTRING

How to: Stand on one leg. Hinge at hip and reach hands toward floor. Other leg extends straight behind you for balance. Keep back straight. Feel stretch in hamstring of standing leg. Return to standing. Builds hamstring strength for pistols!

Reps: 3-4 x 8 per leg

Deep Goblet Squats ‚ñº

üèãÔ∏è WEIGHTED

How to: Hold dumbbell or kettlebell at chest level. Squat down as DEEP as you possibly can. Hold bottom position for a second. Stand up. This builds ankle and hip mobility needed for deep pistol squats. Go ass-to-grass!

Reps: 3 x 10 reps

Deep Squat Hold ‚ñº

üßò MOBILITY

How to: Squat down as deep as possible with heels on ground. Try to keep chest up. Hold this position for 2 minutes total (can break into segments). Can hold onto something for balance. CRUCIAL for pistol squat mobility!

Duration: 2 minutes total

90/90 Hip Stretches ‚ñº

üîÑ HIP OPENER

How to: Sit with both legs bent at 90 degrees - one in front, one behind. Lean forward over front leg. Hold 1 minute. Switch sides. Opens your hips in a controlled way despite your hypermobility. Do this daily!

Duration: 1 minute per side

Cossack Squats ‚ñº

‚ÜîÔ∏è SIDE SQUAT

How to: Stand with wide stance. Shift weight to one leg and squat down on that side. Other leg stays straight with toes pointing up. Rock side to side. Builds hip and ankle mobility for pistol squats.

Reps: 10 per side

Wall Ankle Mobilization ‚ñº

ü¶∂ ANKLE MOBILITY

How to: Face wall with one foot forward. Drive knee toward wall while keeping heel down. Pull back. Repeat 15 times per ankle. This is CRITICAL for pistol squat depth! Do this daily for best results.

Reps: 15 per ankle
